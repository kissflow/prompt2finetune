Prompting Strategies

1. Zero-Shot Prompting

Definition

Ask the model to perform a task without providing any examples. The model relies entirely on its pre-training knowledge.

Example Prompt

Summarize the following review:

"This vacuum cleaner is amazing! 
Lightweight, powerful, and the 
battery lasts forever. My floors 
have never been cleaner."

Output

The review praises a vacuum cleaner for being lightweight, powerful, and having excellent battery life, resulting in exceptionally clean floors.

When to use: Simple, well-defined tasks where the model has strong general knowledge. Ideal for basic summarization, translation, or straightforward Q&A.

2. Few-Shot Prompting

Definition

Provide 2-5 example input-output pairs to guide the model's behavior and demonstrate the expected format.

Example Prompt

Summarize product reviews:

Review: Battery dies in an hour, camera is blurry.
Summary: Poor battery life and camera quality.

Review: Fast laptop, lightweight, stunning screen.
Summary: Fast, lightweight, excellent display.

Review: This phone has great features but overheats.
Summary:

Output

Good features but has overheating issues.

When to use: When you need specific output format, working with domain-specific patterns, or maintaining consistent tone and style.

3. Self Reflection

Definition

The model generates an initial response, critiques its own output, and then produces a refined version based on that critique.

Example Prompt

Summarize this review using self-reflection:

Review: "This laptop is decent but has issues..."

Steps:
1. INITIAL SUMMARY: Write a draft summary
2. SELF-CRITIQUE: Identify what's missing or unclear
3. REFINED SUMMARY: Provide an improved version

Output (Refined)

After self-critique, the model produces a more comprehensive summary that includes all key details, emotional tone, and balanced perspective.

When to use: High-stakes content where quality is critical - technical documentation, creative writing, complex analysis.

4. Chain of Thought

Definition

Require the model to explicitly show its step-by-step reasoning process before providing the final answer.

Example Prompt

Summarize this review, thinking step by step:

Review: "This vacuum cleaner works well but..."

Let's think step by step:
1. Identify the product being reviewed
2. List positive and negative attributes
3. Combine into a concise summary

Output

1. Product: Vacuum cleaner
2. Positive: Works well; Negative: Heavy, short battery
3. Summary: Effective vacuum with decent performance but suffers from weight and battery life issues.

When to use: Complex reasoning tasks, math problems, logic puzzles, debugging, or when the reasoning process is as important as the answer.

5. Tree of Thoughts

Definition

Explore multiple reasoning paths simultaneously, evaluate different approaches, and select the best solution.

Concept Example

Problem: Summarize a complex multi-faceted review

Approach 1: Focus on technical aspects ‚Üí Evaluate
Approach 2: Emphasize user experience ‚Üí Evaluate  
Approach 3: Balance features and value ‚Üí Evaluate

Select best approach based on evaluation

Result

The model explores multiple summarization strategies, evaluates each path, and selects the most comprehensive approach that balances all aspects.

When to use: Strategic planning, creative brainstorming, complex optimization, or when multiple valid solutions exist. Note: 10-50x more expensive.

6. Meta Prompting

Definition

Assign the model an explicit role with structured instructions and specific output format requirements.

Example Prompt

You are an expert product review analyst with 
10 years of experience. Your goal is to provide 
concise summaries focusing on key positive and 
negative aspects.

Review: "This smartphone has..."

Provide summary in format:
Summary: [Your analysis]
Rating: [1-5 stars]
Recommendation: [Yes/No with reason]

Output

Summary: Excellent display and camera but poor battery life.
Rating: 3/5 stars
Recommendation: Yes for photography enthusiasts, No for heavy users.

When to use: Production systems, API integrations, maintaining consistent behavior across interactions, or when specific output formats are required.
